Smart Signals Onboarding


Company: TJIP B.V | Delft NL
Duration: 1.5 months



Introduction

Smart Signals is an application that allows users to import government data and gain clear insights into their financial situation. As part of the project, I am involved in creating the Smart Signals application, though many aspects remain confidential. Therefore, I will focus on the onboarding flow here, which is a crucial part of any productâ€”especially one related to finance, a topic many users may not be familiar with. A great onboarding experience is essential to guide users and help them engage with the product effectively.

Challenge & Goal

  • Create an onboarding flow for product Smart Signals
  • Need to align with the TJIP branding
  • Keep it simple but provide enough guidance

My role

  • Research
  • UX
  • UI

My Process

Discover

For this project, unfortunately, TJIP doesn't have the budget for me to recruit users and do interviews. Therefore, desk research was conducted. There are two parts:

  • Understanding the behavior of Dutch people towards digital financial applications
  • Researching the existing examples of onboarding flow for digital applications

My findings:

  • Close to 70% of all people would like to be instructed about online safety. (data gathered from CBS*)
  • The biggest concern for Dutch people is the misuse of their banking data and personal data. Over 25% worry about how secure their data is. (data gathered from CBS)
  • Best to use the right tone of voice to give it a human touch.
  • For onboarding, it is important to "frontload the value" so the users can have a better view of what they can expect in this application.

*CBS (Centraal Bureau voor de Statistiek) is a statistical agency in the Netherlands, CBS provides insight into social issues with reliable statistical information and data.

This is part of the results from usability testing regarding the color of primary and secondary buttons. The test was conducted both in person and online.

Define

After analyzing the data from the discovery phase, the design goal is formulated as:

"I want to create an onboarding flow that enables trust and guidance by frontloading the value Smart Signals and using the right tone of voice."

Ideation

After having a clear design goal, I mapped out the user journey and made many sketches and wireframes to generate the initial design.

This is part of the wireframes that I made for the Smart Signals onboarding flow.

Validation

I tested the wireframe and Lo-fi prototype internally with my colleagues. Based on the feedback, I iterated the designs.

My findings:

  • The "data uploading step" is not needed in the onboarding flow (although it is nice to show the user signals related to their finance data when they first log in to the application). However, through discussion, I consider it is better to encourage the users to upload their data after the account is made.
  • Through brainstorming, I came up with the idea of providing the user with a taste of how the signal works. (Users can provide simple data, for example, address, and the system can calculate the signal related to your house based on the API we have) Therefore, this feature is added to the onboarding flow to give users a peek into what they can get out of this product.

The idea of providing the user with a taste of how the signal works. Users can provide simple data, and we can calculate related signals for them.

Final Design

  • Smooth and simple onboarding
  • Frontload the value of the Smart Signals application
  • Guide the user through the main feature of the application

Next Step

The onboarding is one part of the Smart Signals application, the next step will be to continue to work on the details inside the application. However, most of the features inside are confidential. Therefore, this is why I selected the onboarding flow to showcase my ability to conduct research, ideation, and translate the concept into a realistic product.
