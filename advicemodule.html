Advice module - ABN

UX & Usability test sessions


Company: TJIP B.V | Client: ABN
Duration: 4 months



Introduction

The Advice module is a product designed to support mortgage advisors write advice reports more efficiently and in a standardized manner. Currently, it takes an average of 20 hours for an advisor to complete a report. To address this, the new design incorporates Machine Learning and AI, which will assist advisors in composing reports faster and more accurately. The first version of the Advice module, a conceptual design, was designed by me after several brainstorming sessions with our client, ABN. After developing this conceptual design, I plan to conduct UX and usability testing with actual mortgage advisors to ensure the design effectively supports their process of writing advice reports. By leveraging AI and Machine Learning, we aim to significantly reduce the time spent on report writing, ultimately enhancing the efficiency and productivity of mortgage advisors. In this page, I would like to introduce the process of my UX and usability test.

Goals

  • Evaluate if the advice module design fits the needs of the users and if it can support their process of writing an advice report.
  • Gather user input to make more accurate design decisions.

My role

  • Research
  • UX
  • UI

My Process

1. Planning

  • Created a step by step test plan

2. Recruit participants

  • 5 mortgage advisors from ABN were recruited with the help from Roland (ABN colleague)

3. UX & Usability test sessions

  • Observation
  • Interviews

4. Analyze

  • Analyzed the results from the user sessions

5. Redesign

  • Proposed redesign based on the findings from the user sessions

Step 4, Analyze: different colors of post-it represent different participants. I documented and categorized their inputs.


If you would like to know more about the research process, please feel free to connect me on LinkedIn.

Conclusion from the user sessions

  • The new application (Advice module) is easy to operate by users. It functions well and is easy to understand by users.
  • The visual style of the application fits with the ABN brand.
  • The current version is not smart enough, which requires the user to answer many similar questions. Therefore, users doubted that the writing process of the advice report would be faster with this new application. This is considered the most important point to be improved. A smarter version of the Advice module which consists of business rules that can support users writing their reports faster will be ideal.
  • Several improvement points can be made for the UX part (please see the Redesign below).

Results

In conclusion, the redesign of the Advice module has successfully reduced the time mortgage advisors spend writing reports, cutting the process from 20 hours to less than 5 hours, based on current estimations. The redesign was thoroughly discussed with the Product Owner, developers, and clients, all of whom were pleased with the improvements in UX and usability. Following these discussions, I created a prioritized list of redesign suggestions and delivered it to the development team for implementation. The project has made significant progress toward enhancing the overall efficiency and user experience for mortgage advisors.
